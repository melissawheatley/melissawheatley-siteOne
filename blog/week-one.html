Squad Goals: A Jumping Off Point for Career Transitions

Starting a new professional journey is not for the faint of heart. While I've done it several times now over the past decade of "adulting," there always seems to be anxiety and stress that go in to both the "lasts" and the "firsts" that all happen in rapid succession.

For those who are unaware, I recently resigned from my position as a Web Content Manager so that I can focus on learning web development and delving even deeper into User Experience design. I'm now an apprentice (read: student) at Nashville Software School. It's very scary to exchange a stable job for six months as a student, but I am confident that this is the next right step for me. And yet, having that confidence in my decision doesn't keep me from moments of doubt. After just my first week of class with almost 30 other people making these same new beginnings, there have been several moments of both certainty and fear.

In part, it can be a bit lonely to begin again. Walking in to a lunch room where you don't anyone isn't any less awkward than it was for me as a sixteen-year-old in a new town. While I do feel these situations are a bit easier now, there is still a sense of wanting to fit in and find someone to connect with. And so, even beyond the coding, I can say that this is my biggest takeaway from my first week at NSS:

Build your support team. Gather your squad. And let them be there for you.

Trying to remember how important it is to build your own community when you don't have colleagues to fall back on.
